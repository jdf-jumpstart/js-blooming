What is direct primary care?

Direct primary care (DPC) is a membership model for primary care. You pay a flat monthly fee. In return, you get a real relationship with a provider who has time for you, and direct access when something comes up.

Primary care without the insurance middleman.

In the traditional model, your provider is paid per visit — by your insurance company. That creates pressure to see as many patients as possible. The result: shorter appointments, longer wait times, and a provider who barely knows your name.

DPC flips the incentive. Instead of billing insurance, your provider charges a flat monthly fee and sees far fewer patients. That extra time translates into longer appointments, same-day access, and a real relationship built over years.

The membership covers all your primary care visits. Between appointments, you can reach your provider directly — by text or phone — without going through a call center.

What changes when you leave the traditional model.

Traditional care
  • 8–12 minute appointments
  • Weeks to get in for a routine visit
  • Provider changes year to year
  • Billed through insurance for every visit
  • Call a phone tree when something comes up
  • Rushed — next patient is already waiting
Direct primary care
  • 30–60 minute appointments
  • Same- or next-day when you need it
  • Same provider, year after year
  • Flat monthly fee — no per-visit billing
  • Text or call your provider directly
  • Unhurried — the time is actually yours

Things people get wrong about DPC.

DPC replaces health insurance.

It replaces the need for insurance in primary care — not for specialists, hospitals, or emergencies. Most members keep a plan for anything beyond primary care.

DPC is only for wealthy people.

At $100/month, it costs less than most gym memberships. Many members drop expensive plans and pair DPC with a high-deductible plan and an HSA.

It's the same as concierge medicine.

Concierge practices layer a fee on top of insurance billing. DPC drops insurance billing entirely — the membership is the only fee.

You still have to wait for appointments.

DPC providers carry a fraction of the patient panel of a traditional practice. Same-day and next-day access is the norm, not the exception.

DPC is not a replacement for insurance.

Membership covers everything in the primary care lane — routine visits, chronic disease management, urgent care, and direct access to your provider. For anything beyond that, you still need coverage. Most DPC members pair their membership with a high-deductible health plan and an HSA, which often costs less combined than traditional insurance alone.

  • Specialist care — billed through your insurance as normal
  • Emergency room and hospital stays
  • Major surgeries and procedures
  • Advanced imaging — MRI, CT (we help coordinate cost)
  • All primary and urgent care visits
  • Wholesale lab work at significantly reduced cost
  • Direct access to your provider, any time

DPC works best when primary care actually matters to you.

People tired of being rushed

If you have ever left an appointment with half your questions unanswered, DPC is built for you.

Those managing chronic conditions

Diabetes, hypertension, thyroid, cholesterol — ongoing management works better with a provider who knows your full picture.

Families who want continuity

The same provider over years. Someone who knows your history, your kids, and what actually matters to your household.

Self-employed and uninsured

DPC paired with a catastrophic plan is often more affordable than traditional insurance, with far better primary care access.

Health-conscious adults

If you think about prevention, lifestyle, and natural options — DPC gives you a provider who thinks the same way.

Anyone who has given up on primary care

If you avoid the doctor because the experience is never worth it, this is the practice that changes that.
